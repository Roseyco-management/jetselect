Board Approval Process Framework

1. Pre-Board Preparation (Weken 1-4)

  • Develop business case: strategic rationale, competitive necessity, market analysis
  • Financial modeling: 5-year TCO, break-even analysis, ROI projections
  • Governance draft: usage policies, controls, compliance framework
  • Risk assessment: identification, mitigation strategies
  • Stakeholder alignment: one-on-one discussions met CEO, CFO, audit committee chair

2. Executive Committee Review (Weken 5-6)

Present to exec committee (CEO, CFO, COO) voor feedback, revisions prior to full board. This pre-filters concerns, builds executive ownership, improves board presentation quality.

3. Audit Committee Deep Dive (Weken 7-8)

Audit committee meets separately to review: governance controls, compliance framework, audit procedures, tax implications, insurance structure. This committee often becomes strongest advocate post-review, supporting full board recommendation.

4. Full Board Presentation (Weken 9-10)

Formal board meeting presentation: executive summary, business case, financials, governance, risk mitigation, recommendations. Allow Q&A, address concerns, build consensus for approval vote.

5. Post-Approval Execution (Weken 11+)

Board resolution documentation, aircraft acquisition, crew hiring, operational setup, board oversight continuation via quarterly reports.

Stakeholder Management Strategy

CEO Alignment

CEO is primary board influencer. Present business case emphasizing: competitive advantage, executive productivity gains, client entertainment capabilities. One-on-one conversation building CEO ownership essential - if CEO champions acquisition, board typically follows. Address CEO concerns privately antes full board. Consider personal use policies for executive alignment.

CFO Engagement

CFO focused on financials: cost certainty, budget impact, capital allocation trade-offs. Provide detailed financial models, sensitivity analysis, comparison vs charter alternatives. CFO must understand and believe in economics - CFO presentation to board critical credibility factor. Review CFO decision frameworks and financing options.

Audit Committee Support

Audit committee reviews governance, controls, compliance. Demonstrate: clear usage policies, expense tracking systems, audit procedures, tax compliance framework, insurance governance. Audit committee becomes strongest governance advocate if convinced controls are robust - explore comprehensive governance frameworks.

Board Member Individual Outreach

Meet individually met each director (especially skeptics) prior to formal presentation. Understand concerns, provide context, address objections. Board members often soften objections if engaged early through one-on-one conversations.

External Auditors

Notify external auditors of aircraft acquisition plan. Discuss accounting treatment (capitalization, depreciation), controls framework, audit implications. External auditors generally supportive if governance is solid - they want proper controls, n√£o oppose ownership per se.

Governance Framework Presentation

Usage Policies

Define: authorized users (CEO, C-suite, specific VPs), approved purposes (business meetings, client entertainment, site visits), prohibited uses (personal vacations absent reimbursement), approval workflows. Make policies clear, enforceable, transparent - demonstrates governance rigor.

Internal Controls

Show: segregation of duties (different people approve trips, manage operations, track expenses), authorization limits (CFO approves routine, CEO approves major), documented approval trails. Board wants assurance controls prevent abuse and ensure accountability. Consider aircraft management companies for operational oversight.

Audit & Reporting

Describe: monthly management review of flight logs vs. stated business purpose, quarterly internal audit sampling (10-15 trips tested), annual comprehensive audit. Show board you'll have eyes on operations continuously, not just annual review - explore governance best practices.

Tax & Compliance

Outline: BTW recovery documentation (flight logs, business justification), fringe benefit taxation (private usage reporting), transfer pricing (if inter-company usage), regulatory compliance (airworthiness, crew licensing). Engage tax advisor to explain framework - demonstrates sophistication. Review ownership structures for optimal setup.

Addressing Board Objections

Objection: "Too Expensive - Why Not Charter?"

Response: Break-even analysis shows ownership economical at 250-300 hours/year. Charter inflexible (availability constraints, short-notice premiums, no privacy). Ownership provides schedule flexibility, custom configuration, always-ready capability critical for C-suite. Compare midsize vs large cabin options for specific needs.

Objection: "Governance Risks?"

Response: Robust controls framework with monthly reviews, quarterly audits, annual comprehensive assessment. Clear policies prevent abuse, expense tracking ensures accountability, regular board reporting maintains oversight. Governance as good as or better than other major corporate assets.

Objection: "Reputational Concerns - Executive Excess?"

Response: Aircraft improves business productivity, competitive necessity (competitors operate jets), recruitment/retention tool for top executives. Usage clearly business-focused, not personal luxury. Proper disclosure in financial reporting demonstrates governance rigor - review personal use guidelines for transparency.

Objection: "Tax Exposure?"

Response: Professional tax structuring optimizes treatment - BTW recovery based on 70%+ business use, fringe benefit tax manageable via proper documentation/reimbursement, transfer pricing arm's-length methodologies. Engage Big Four tax advisor; professional opinion alleviates concerns.

Objection: "Alternative Solutions?"

Response: Evaluated charter (inflexible, expensive at volume), fractional ownership (limited availability, shared aircraft), jet cards (inflexible, high costs). Ownership optimal given company's mission profile and utilization projections.

Board Presentation - Execution

Presentation Structure

  • Executive Summary (1 slide): What (midsize jet acquisition), Why (business productivity), Cost (‚Ç¨25M acquisition + ‚Ç¨2M/year operating), Benefit (break-even 250+ hours/year)
  • Business Case (3-4 slides): Strategic rationale, competitive analysis, utilization projections, ROI
  • Financial Analysis (4-5 slides): TCO comparison, break-even, sensitivity analysis, budget impact
  • Governance Framework (3 slides): Policies, controls, audit procedures, reporting
  • Risk Management (2 slides): Insurance, operational, tax/compliance risks + mitigation
  • Recommendation (1 slide): Clear call to vote for approval

Presentation Tips

  • Keep slides clean, data-driven, not cluttered
  • Lead with business case, not airplane details (board cares about business impact)
  • Use third-party validation (consultant analysis, industry benchmarks)
  • Anticipate top 3-5 questions, have data ready
  • Bring aviation expert to Q&A for detailed technical questions
  • Avoid jargon - explain aircraft terminology for non-aviation directors
  • Allow 20-30 minutes presentation + 20-30 minutes Q&A

Veelgestelde Vragen - Board Approval

Hoe lang duurt board approval typisch?

4-6 maanden: 4-6 weken voorbereiding, 2-3 weken exec committee/audit review, 2 weken full board approval, 4-8 weken closing. Dutch board meetings quarterly dus timing important - if you miss one meeting, wait another 3 maanden voor next cycle.

Welke documentatie moet ik voorbereiding?

Business case (strategic rationale), financial analysis (TCO, break-even), governance framework (policies, controls), risk assessment (mitigation), presentation deck, independent consultant analysis (optional but recommended), preliminary aircraft proposal (model, specifications, costs).

Moet ik externe advisors inhuren?

Highly recommended: aviation consultant (‚Ç¨15-30K) provides independent financial analysis, aviation tax advisor (‚Ç¨10-20K) addresses tax concerns, legal advisor for contracts. External validation dramatically improves board confidence; costs minimal relative to ‚Ç¨25M acquisition.

Hoe handle ik skeptische board members?

Individual meetings pre-board: understand concerns, provide context. Address objections with data, third-party validation, mitigation strategies. Often skepticism dissolves with personal engagement and credible information. Make skeptics allies by incorporating valid feedback into proposal.

Wat als board votes nee?

Analyze feedback: financial concerns? present revised TCO. Governance concerns? enhance controls. Reputational issues? emphasize business necessity. Can resubmit following quarter with improvements. Alternatively: lease aircraft (capital-lighter) as compromise solution.

Hoe manage ik post-approval board oversight?

Quarterly board reports: utilization metrics, budget vs actual, KPIs, safety/compliance status. Annual comprehensive review. Designate aircraft steering committee (CEO, CFO, General Counsel) meeting quarterly. Continuous improvement based on board feedback.

Welke governance requirements zijn essentieel?

Written usage policy (authorized users, purposes, approval workflow), budget oversight (annual operating budget, variance monitoring), internal controls (segregation of duties, authorization limits), audit procedures (monthly reviews, quarterly testing, annual comprehensive), expense tracking, board reporting (quarterly summaries).

Hoe present ik financial case credibly?

Use conservative assumptions (higher costs, lower utilization vs optimistic), show sensitivity analysis (what if utilization ¬±25%, fuel costs rise, maintenance costs spike), provide independent consultant analysis, compare ownership vs charter realistically. Credibility comes from balanced, realistic financials, not best-case scenarios.

Wat is realisticboard utilization projection?

Conservative: 200-300 hours/year assuming some underutilization. Aggressive: 400+ hours/year (assumes consistent demand). Most companies fall 300-350 hours/year once established. Underestimate rather than overestimate - better to exceed projections dan disappoint.

Hoe address ik tax compliance concerns?

Engage Big Four tax advisor (PwC, EY, Deloitte) to present framework: BTW recovery strategy (document business use >70%), fringe benefit taxation (reporting/withholding), transfer pricing (if applicable). Professional validation addresses board concerns that tax exposure is managed proactively.
